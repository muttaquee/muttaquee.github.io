Imagine buying a home without ever stepping inside it. For many people, particularly international buyers or those relocating for work, this is already a reality. While online listings, photographs, and video walkthroughs have made property searching more accessible, they still leave an important gap: the absence of human interaction.

> **The Bigger Picture:** AI-driven MetaHumans have the potential to transform virtual property tours from passive viewing experiences into interactive conversations. By combining immersive Virtual Reality with intelligent digital humans, future property buyers could explore homes, ask questions naturally, and receive personalised guidance—all within a single virtual environment.

In this blog, I want to explore how AI-driven MetaHumans could bridge that gap by acting as intelligent virtual property guides within immersive Virtual Reality environments.

## The Problem: Property Tours Are Still Limited

The property industry has embraced digital transformation, but much of the viewing experience remains passive. Buyers are often expected to browse through static images or pre-recorded videos, with questions answered later through emails or scheduled calls.

Even live virtual tours can feel restrictive. Once the session ends, so does the opportunity to explore and ask questions.

The experience is informative, but it rarely feels interactive.

## The Opportunity: Bringing AI into Virtual Property Tours

![Metahuman in VR interacting with the user](/assets/uploads/link.png)

Virtual Reality allows potential buyers to walk through a property as if they were physically there. Instead of viewing a room from a fixed camera angle, they can freely explore the environment, inspect details, and experience the property's layout at their own pace.

Now imagine combining that immersive experience with an AI-driven MetaHuman.

Rather than navigating the property alone, visitors could be welcomed by a digital guide capable of interacting naturally throughout the tour. Instead of simply displaying information, the MetaHuman becomes an active participant in the experience.

For example, it could:

- Welcome visitors as they enter the property.
- Answer questions about individual rooms and features.
- Highlight key selling points based on the visitor's interests.
- Provide guidance throughout the virtual tour in real time.

The goal is not to replace human estate agents, but to make virtual property viewing more engaging, informative, and accessible.

---

## From Research Idea to Working Prototype

This vision became the foundation of one of my recent research projects. As part of my ongoing PhD research, I designed and developed an AI-driven MetaHuman integrated into an immersive VR property tour.

The prototype combines several technologies, including:

- Unreal Engine MetaHuman technology for creating a realistic digital human.
- Conversational AI to enable natural interaction.
- A Virtual Reality environment that allows users to explore a property while communicating with the MetaHuman in real time.

Rather than focusing solely on visual realism, the project investigates how conversational intelligence and immersive interaction can work together to improve the overall user experience.

---

## What Did We Learn?

The project represents an early step towards understanding how users interact with intelligent digital humans during immersive property tours.

Our early evaluation explored how participants perceived the experience, particularly in relation to realism, engagement, and interaction quality. While there is still significant work ahead, the initial findings provide encouraging evidence that AI-driven MetaHumans have the potential to make virtual property viewing feel more natural and engaging.

Like any emerging technology, there are also important questions surrounding trust, transparency, and user expectations. These are challenges that will continue to shape my research as the project develops.

---

## Looking Ahead

Although this prototype focuses on real estate, the underlying concept extends far beyond property tours. Similar AI MetaHumans could one day assist visitors in museums, guide patients through healthcare services, support students in virtual learning environments, or welcome customers into immersive retail experiences.

What excites me most is not simply creating realistic digital humans, but designing experiences where AI can communicate naturally and meaningfully with people inside immersive environments.

If you are interested in the technical design, implementation, and early evaluation of this prototype, you can explore the full details in my ACM IMX 2026 Work-in-Progress paper, **AI-Driven MetaHuman for Immersive VR Property Tours: Design and Early Evaluation**.
