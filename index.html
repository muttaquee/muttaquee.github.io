01 — About

My journey

I'm a final-year PhD researcher at Teesside University, working where artificial intelligence, virtual reality, and human–computer interaction meet. My thesis explores how AI-driven MetaHumans, photorealistic real-time virtual humans built in Unreal Engine, can deepen immersive brand engagement. I combine real-time character technology, conversational and generative AI, and rigorous user studies to understand how virtual humans shape attention, trust, and emotional response.

Looking ahead, my research vision centres on the convergence of immersive technology, AI-driven virtual humans, digital twins, and the metaverse, and how these can reshape sectors such as marketing, education, healthcare, and entertainment. I want to understand how intelligent, photorealistic virtual humans and immersive environments can create seamless experiences across physical and virtual spaces, support data-driven decision-making, and surface the ethical, social, and technical questions these technologies raise.

As I complete my PhD, I'm eager to carry this work into collaborative research and industry partnerships that move immersive AI beyond static simulations towards genuinely interactive, human-centred experiences.
